Perfume is a mixture of fragrant essential oils or aroma compounds,
fixatives and solvents,
usually in liquid form, used to give the human body, animals, food,
objects, and living-spaces an agreeable scent.

Ancient texts and archaeological excavations show
the use of perfumes in some of the earliest
human civilizations. Modern perfumery began
in the late 19th century with the
commercial synthesis of aroma compounds such as vanillin or coumarin,
which allowed for the composition of perfumes with smells

previously unattainable solely from natural aromatics.
